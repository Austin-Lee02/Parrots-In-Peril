Blue-throated Macaw
Ara glaucogularis
🌟 Spotted at the Toronto Zoo!
Critically
Endangered
© Wikimedia Commons / CC BY-SA
🤩 Cool Fact
This bird was thought to be EXTINCT until rediscovered in the wild in 1992! Today, only about 100–150 still survive in Bolivia — fewer than the number of students in most schools.
⚠️
Main Threat
Cattle ranching is destroying its habitat, and it has been heavily targeted by the illegal pet trade.
🔇 Audio recordings of this species are extremely scarce — fewer than 5 exist worldwide. This is partly a reflection of how rare and inaccessible this bird has become due to illegal trapping and habitat loss.
Red-and-green Macaw
Ara chloropterus
🌟 Spotted at the Toronto Zoo!
Locally
Threatened
🎮 Which one is the REAL macaw? Tap to find out!
Photo © Wikimedia Commons / CC BY-SA · Illustration by student artist
🤩 Cool Fact
The LARGEST macaw species in the world — its wingspan can reach up to 125 cm! That's taller than most grade 6 students standing up.
⚠️
Main Threat
Deforestation across South America is shrinking its habitat, and many are captured for the illegal pet trade.
Spix's Macaw
Cyanopsitta spixii
Extinct
in the Wild
© Wikimedia Commons / CC BY-SA
🤩 Cool Fact
This is the real bird that inspired the movie Rio — and sadly, it is now COMPLETELY GONE from the wild. Only ~180 survive in captivity, and a breeding program is working to bring it back.
⚠️
Main Threat
The illegal pet trade and deforestation wiped out the entire wild population by 2000.
🔇 Only 3 audio recordings of this species are known to exist — a heartbreaking reminder of how close to total extinction it truly is.
Kākāpō
Strigops habroptilus
Critically
Endangered
© Wikimedia Commons / CC BY-SA
🤩 Cool Fact
The world's only FLIGHTLESS parrot — and it smells like flowers! Only about 250 are alive today, all on three protected predator-free islands in New Zealand.
⚠️
Main Threat
Invasive predators — rats, cats, and stoats — brought to New Zealand by humans have almost wiped it out entirely.
African Grey Parrot
Psittacus erithacus
Endangered
© Wikimedia Commons / CC BY-SA
🤩 Cool Fact
One of the smartest animals on Earth — African Greys can learn hundreds of words, count objects, and even understand the concept of zero! Yet over 1 MILLION have been taken from the wild for the pet trade.
⚠️
Main Threat
The most heavily trafficked bird on Earth. The illegal pet trade has caused catastrophic population collapse across Central Africa in the past 50 years.

How YOU Can Help!

Small actions make a HUGE difference for parrots everywhere.

🚫
Never buy wild-caught birdsIf you want a pet bird, adopt from a rescue or certified breeder — never from someone who caught it in the wild.
🌳
Support rainforest protectionTropical forests are parrot homes. Donate to or fundraise for organizations that protect them.
📢
Spread the word!Tell your friends and family what you learned today. Awareness leads to action!
♻️
Reduce your carbon footprintClimate change worsens habitat loss worldwide. Small changes at home add up globally.
🦜
Support the Toronto ZooThis zoo runs real conservation and captive breeding programs for critically endangered species like these.
